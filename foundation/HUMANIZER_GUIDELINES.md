# Humanizer Guidelines

Every piece of content generated by this engine must pass through a humanizer review before it is published. This document defines what that means, why it matters, and how to apply it.

---

## Why This Exists

AI-generated content has a recognizable signature — predictable structure, sterile phrasing, overuse of certain transitions, and a lack of real voice. Publishing content that reads like it came from a machine undermines credibility across all five brands, particularly Leon Coe's personal brand and Amplified Exec where trust is the primary currency.

This is not about hiding that AI was used. It's about ensuring the content reflects a real human perspective, passes the "would a person actually write this" test, and adds genuine value rather than just filling space.

---

## The Humanizer Checklist

Run every piece of content through this checklist before approving for publish.

### Voice & Tone
- [ ] Does this sound like the brand it's attributed to? (Cross-reference `BRAND_VOICES.md`)
- [ ] Are there any phrases that feel generic or "AI-written"? (See banned phrases below)
- [ ] Is there at least one specific, concrete example or observation — not just abstract claims?
- [ ] Does the opening hook feel like something a real person would actually say?
- [ ] Is the CTA natural, not robotic?

### Structure
- [ ] Does the structure feel intentional, not formulaic?
- [ ] Are transitions varied — not all "Additionally," "Furthermore," "Moreover"?
- [ ] Are sentences varied in length? (Mix of short punchy lines and longer explanations)
- [ ] Does it avoid the "3 reasons why / 5 things you need to know" pattern unless it genuinely serves the content?

### Substance
- [ ] Is every claim grounded — in research, in experience, or in a named source?
- [ ] Has anything been removed that was vague filler with no real information?
- [ ] Are any statistics cited accurately with their source?
- [ ] Has the "workslop" check passed — would a senior professional be proud to share this?

### Brand Specifics
- [ ] For **Amplified Exec**: Has a human reviewed this before scheduling? Is every claim sourced?
- [ ] For **Leon Coe personal**: Does this sound like Leon specifically — opinionated, direct, grounded?
- [ ] For **AI First Work**: Is there at least one actionable "do this" element — a prompt, a workflow step, a template?
- [ ] For **Houston AI Club**: Does it tie to community or events in some way?
- [ ] For **Amplify Intelligence**: Is there a framework, outcome, or implementation detail — not just concepts?

## Banned Phrases

These phrases are flags for AI-generated content. Remove or rewrite any of the following:

**Openings to avoid:**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "As AI continues to transform..."
- "It's no secret that..."
- "Now more than ever..."
- "In this article, we will explore..."

**Filler transitions to avoid:**
- "Furthermore," / "Moreover," / "Additionally," (use sparingly — one max per piece)
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "When all is said and done..."

**Hype language — banned across all brands:**
- "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing" / "Disruptive"
- "Transform everything" / "The future is here"
- "Unlock the power of..."
- "Harness the potential of..."
- "Cutting-edge" / "State-of-the-art"

**Vague claims — rewrite with specifics:**
- "Many companies are using AI to..." → name one, describe what they're doing
- "Studies show that..." → cite the actual study
- "Experts agree that..." → name an expert or cite a source
- "AI can help you..." → say specifically how, with what tool, in what workflow

## Humanizer Pass — How to Apply It

### For Long-Form Articles

1. Read the full draft once without editing — get a feel for where it falls flat
2. Rewrite the opening hook from scratch in the brand's voice
3. Cut any paragraph that is pure filler — if it says nothing specific, remove it
4. Add one concrete real-world example or scenario if none exists
5. Vary sentence length deliberately in at least two sections
6. Replace all banned phrases
7. Rewrite the CTA in the brand's natural voice
8. Read the closing paragraph out loud — if it sounds like a press release, rewrite it

### For Short-Form Posts

1. Read it and ask: "Would I stop scrolling for this?"
2. If the hook is generic, rewrite it — the first line is everything
3. Make sure there's one specific, concrete insight — not just a broad observation
4. Check that the CTA feels natural, not like a form letter
5. Remove any banned phrases
6. Read out loud in the brand's voice — does it sound right?

## Humanizer Intensity Levels

Not every piece needs the same level of humanization. Use these levels as a guide:

| Level | When to Use | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **Light** | Houston AI Club, AI First Work short posts | Fix banned phrases, punch up the hook, verify CTA |
| **Medium** | Amplify Intelligence articles, Leon Coe posts | Full checklist, rewrite weak sections, add concrete examples |
| **Heavy** | Amplified Exec memos, Leon Coe long-form | Human editorial review required, full rewrite of any generic sections, source verification |

---

## The "Workslop" Test

Before approving any piece for publish, ask this question:

> *"If a senior professional in this field read this, would they think it added genuine value — or would they roll their eyes and keep scrolling?"*

If the honest answer is "they'd roll their eyes" — it goes back for another pass. No exceptions.

This standard protects the credibility of all five brands and positions the engine's output as signal, not noise.
