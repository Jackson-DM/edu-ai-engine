# Brand Voices & CTA Map

This document defines the voice, tone, stance, CTA targets, proof types, and risk posture for each of the five brands served by the edu-ai-engine. Every piece of generated content must pass through the lens of its target brand before publishing.

---

## Houston AI Club

**Voice:** Approachable, community-driven, inclusive, energetic
**Tone:** Warm, welcoming, conversational — like a knowledgeable friend in the room
**Stance:** "We're all figuring this out together" — community-first, never elitist
**Proof Type:** Community proof — member wins, event highlights, speaker recaps, attendance numbers
**Risk Posture:** Low — can be more experimental and casual; less formal review needed
**CTA Targets:**
- Event registrations
- Page follows
- Community membership
- Meetup RSVPs

**Example CTA phrases:**
- "Join us at the next meetup →"
- "Follow Houston AI Club for weekly AI insights"
- "Share this with someone breaking into AI"

**Content that fits this brand:**
- Event recaps and speaker highlights
- Beginner-friendly AI explainers
- Local Houston AI ecosystem news
- Tool demos and hands-on walkthroughs
- Member spotlights

---

## Amplify Intelligence

**Voice:** Authoritative, forward-thinking, results-oriented, precise
**Tone:** Professional but not stiff — confident and implementation-focused
**Stance:** "Here's exactly how to do it" — practitioner credibility, no fluff
**Proof Type:** Client proof — outcomes, case studies, frameworks, ROI data
**Risk Posture:** Medium — content should be substantive and source-grounded; avoid unverified claims
**CTA Targets:**
- Discovery call bookings
- Lead magnet downloads (scorecards, templates, playbooks)
- Workshop registrations
- Coaching inquiries

**Example CTA phrases:**
- "Book a discovery call →"
- "Download the AI Implementation Scorecard"
- "Get the playbook →"

**Content that fits this brand:**
- AI implementation roadmaps
- CodeAlign / AI-assisted development
- Enterprise AI adoption frameworks
- ROI and measurement guides
- Agent deployment and governance

---

## Amplified Exec

**Voice:** Concise, high-stakes, action-oriented, board-ready
**Tone:** Executive — crisp, no jargon, no fluff. Every sentence earns its place.
**Stance:** "Here's what this means for your organization and what to do next"
**Proof Type:** Research proof — cite reputable sources (McKinsey, WEF, MIT Sloan, HBR)
**Risk Posture:** High — must be conservative, well-sourced, and legally clean. No speculation.
**CTA Targets:**
- Policy template downloads
- Executive briefing subscriptions
- Speaking/consulting inquiries

**Example CTA phrases:**
- "Reply 'POLICY' to get the 2-page AI agent governance template"
- "Forward this to your leadership team"
- "Subscribe to the Amplified Exec briefing →"

**Content that fits this brand:**
- AI governance and risk frameworks
- Board-level AI strategy
- C-suite adoption playbooks
- AI KPI design
- Regulatory and compliance landscape

## Leon Coe (Personal)

**Voice:** Candid, opinionated, experienced, direct
**Tone:** Real — like Leon is talking to you over coffee, not presenting to a boardroom
**Stance:** Contrarian but evidence-based — challenges hype, backs claims with real experience
**Proof Type:** Personal proof — Leon's own experiments, wins, failures, observations
**Risk Posture:** Medium-low — can be opinionated and bold, but must be grounded and accurate
**CTA Targets:**
- Profile follows
- DM conversations
- Consulting/speaking inquiries
- Community referrals to Houston AI Club

**Example CTA phrases:**
- "What's your experience with this? Drop it in the comments."
- "Follow for more takes like this"
- "DM me 'ROADMAP' and I'll send you the framework we use"

**Content that fits this brand:**
- POV posts and contrarian takes
- "What I learned from..." narratives
- Tool experiment results
- Implementation stories
- State-of-AI commentary

## AI First Work

**Voice:** Practical, energetic, hands-on, encouraging
**Tone:** Like a coach — direct, actionable, motivating. Makes AI feel accessible not intimidating.
**Stance:** "You can do this, and here's exactly how" — tool-agnostic, workflow-first
**Proof Type:** Process proof — step-by-step workflows, templates, prompt libraries, before/after examples
**Risk Posture:** Low-medium — can be bold and tactical; avoid vendor bias toward any single tool
**CTA Targets:**
- Course enrollments (aifirstwork.com)
- Email list opt-ins
- Template downloads
- Challenge completions

**Example CTA phrases:**
- "Try this workflow today →"
- "Grab the prompt template below"
- "Enroll in the AI First Work course →"
- "Follow for one practical AI tip per day"

**Content that fits this brand:**
- Workflow and productivity systems
- Tool comparisons (Claude vs ChatGPT vs Gemini)
- Prompt templates and frameworks
- Role-based AI use cases
- "AI habit" building content

## Cross-Brand Rules

1. **Never publish the same article verbatim across brands.** Each piece must be adapted — at minimum the hook, CTA, and proof type must change.
2. **Humanizer pass is mandatory** before any piece goes to publish. See `HUMANIZER_GUIDELINES.md`.
3. **Amplified Exec content must be reviewed by Leon** before scheduling — no exceptions.
4. **Tool mentions should be brand-appropriate** — AI First Work stays tool-agnostic, Amplify Intelligence can be specific, Houston AI Club can demo specific tools.
5. **No hype language** across any brand. Words to avoid: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruptive," "transform everything," "the future is here."
