# Content Pillars & Topic Taxonomy

The edu-ai-engine organizes all content around five core pillars. Every article and post generated by this engine maps to one pillar. This keeps content focused, measurable, and strategically coherent across all five brands.

---

## The Five Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Implementation Playbooks
*The "how" — practical, step-by-step guidance for actually deploying AI*

**Why it matters:** Most organizations are stuck between pilot and scale. This pillar owns the implementation gap — the space between "we tried AI" and "AI is running our workflows."

**Topic areas:**
- AI adoption roadmaps and maturity models
- AI agent deployment and governance
- Pilot-to-production frameworks
- AI evaluation and quality measurement
- Operating model design for AI teams
- Change management and AI culture
- ROI measurement and KPI design
- Responsible AI checklists

**Best-fit brands:** Amplify Intelligence, Amplified Exec, Leon Coe
**Content formats:** Long-form articles, playbook carousels, CEO memos, downloadable templates

---

### Pillar 2 — AI-First Work Systems
*The "habits" — how individuals and teams work better with AI every day*

**Why it matters:** AI literacy is becoming table stakes. This pillar serves professionals who want to build repeatable, high-quality AI workflows into their daily work — not just experiment occasionally.

**Topic areas:**
- Personal AI productivity systems
- Prompt engineering techniques and frameworks
- Tool selection and comparison (Claude vs ChatGPT vs Gemini, etc.)
- Role-based AI workflows (marketer, developer, executive, writer, etc.)
- AI habit building and consistency
- Managing AI outputs — editing, evaluating, humanizing
- Token economics and cost-aware prompting
- Building a personal AI toolkit

**Best-fit brands:** AI First Work, Leon Coe
**Content formats:** Short-form posts, prompt template carousels, weekly email lessons, how-to guides

---

### Pillar 3 — Build & Ship
*The "builder lane" — for developers and technical practitioners building with AI*

**Why it matters:** The explosion of AI tooling has created massive demand for practical build guidance. This pillar serves people who are actually coding with AI — cursor workflows, agent architectures, MCP integrations, and more.

**Topic areas:**
- AI-assisted development workflows (Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf, Codex CLI)
- Model Context Protocol (MCP) — what it is, how to use it, what to build
- Multi-agent system design and orchestration
- RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) — concepts and implementation
- Prompt-to-code patterns and maintainability
- AI code review and quality standards
- Building with LLM APIs (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google)
- Vibe coding — what works, what doesn't
- Local model deployment and fine-tuning basics

**Best-fit brands:** Houston AI Club, Amplify Intelligence (CodeAlign), Leon Coe
**Content formats:** Long-form technical articles, demo recaps, GitHub walkthroughs, build-night posts

---

### Pillar 4 — Leadership, Risk & Governance
*The "guardrails" — helping leaders navigate AI responsibly and strategically*

**Why it matters:** As AI moves from experiments to enterprise infrastructure, leaders need frameworks for governance, risk management, and strategic decision-making — not just enthusiasm.

**Topic areas:**
- AI governance frameworks and policy templates
- Responsible AI and bias mitigation
- AI risk registers and compliance
- Board-level AI strategy
- Data privacy and IP considerations in AI workflows
- Regulatory landscape (EU AI Act, FTC, copyright)
- AI vendor selection and due diligence
- Building an AI-ready culture
- AI ethics for practitioners

**Best-fit brands:** Amplified Exec, Amplify Intelligence
**Content formats:** Executive memos, policy templates, long-form governance guides, research-backed posts

---

### Pillar 5 — Community & Ecosystem
*The "social proof" — spotlights, recaps, and ecosystem curation*

**Why it matters:** Community content builds trust and belonging. It also drives event attendance, page follows, and word-of-mouth growth — especially for Houston AI Club.

**Topic areas:**
- Event recaps and speaker highlights
- Member/community spotlights
- Houston AI ecosystem news
- Curated learning paths and resources
- AI tool and product launch coverage
- Trending topics in the AI community
- "What we're watching" curation posts

**Best-fit brands:** Houston AI Club (primary), Leon Coe (secondary)
**Content formats:** Recap document posts, short community updates, resource roundups, event promos

## Engagement Type Definitions

- **Evergreen** — Consistently high engagement regardless of news cycle. Always worth publishing.
- **Trending** — Currently high buzz. Prioritize now, reassess in 6 months.
- **Emerging** — Growing but not yet mainstream. Plant the flag early.
